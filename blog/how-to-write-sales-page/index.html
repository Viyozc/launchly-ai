How to Write a Sales Page: The Structure That Sells

Updated 2026-07-22 · Launchly team

Structure beats cleverness

Buyers scan in a predictable order. Give them the structure they expect and they convert. A free sales-page conversion audit scores all seven elements so you know which section is leaking sales.

Above the fold: outcome first

In five seconds a stranger must know what this is, who it's for, and what changes for them. Lead with the outcome, not features. "Write and sell your first course in 30 days" beats "Online course platform".

The pain, stated specifically

Name the exact frustration: "you've recorded 12 lessons and sold 3". Specific pain is relatable; vague benefit is forgettable.

Proof before the pitch

Claims without proof are noise. Real screenshots, named testimonials, and numbers ("student went from $0 to $2k/mo") reduce risk. The more expensive the course, the more proof you need.

Benefits, then the CTA

Rewrite bullets from the buyer's view: "know exactly what to film next" not "12 video modules". One primary CTA, repeated at top, middle, and bottom. A weak or missing CTA is the top conversion killer.
